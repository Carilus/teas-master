What Is The TEAS Test?

As mentioned above, TEAS stand for Test of Essential Academic Skills. The TEAS test is meant for nursing students who dream of joining nursing school. It gauges the candidates' academic potential and forms part of the application process that nursing students should undertake.

TEAS test is comparable to HESI A2 (Health Education Systems Incorporated Admissions) and NCLEX (National Council Licensure Examination) . Various universities use TEAS tests as part of the nursing school application process.


What Is The TEAS Test Used For? ?

The Test of Essential Academic Skills (TEAS) assesses students' readiness for academic programs. Most universities use as a benchmark since it's believed that students who score better in the TEAS exam will perform excellently in higher education.

The four-section exam evaluates mathematics, reading, language usage, and science proficiency. In addition, it considers problem-solving, analytical, and critical thinking skills.

Apart from determining students' eligibility for admission into nursing school, the TEAS test is also used in the distribution of financial aid and the award of scholarships. Involved parties need assurance that the prospective students will perform well academically. Therefore, the TEAS test establishes students' suitability for any health-related course.

What is the Format of TEAS test?

What is the Format of TEAS test? The TEAS test is a standardized exam comprising 170 multiple-choice questions with a time limit of 209 minutes. The computer-based exam is further divided into four sections which include:

  • English and Language Usage (28 questions for 28 minutes)
  • Science (53 questions, 63 minutes)
  • Math (34 questions, 54 minutes)
  • Reading (53 questions, 64 minutes)

The reading section of the TEAS test is designed to evaluate the ability of the student to comprehend and analyze the written passage, while math asses basic mathematical skills like algebra, measurement conversation, and geometry. On the other hand, the assessment of science with the TEAS test is based on the student's general knowledge of chemistry, physics, biology, physiology, and anatomy. Finally, English and language check sentence structure, punctuation, and grammar proficiency.

7 Effective TEAS Test Preparation Tips For Success

Proper TEAS test preparation will ensure you pass the first attempt and increase your chances of getting into your desired program. Here are some TEAS test tips to help you prepare and pass the first attempt:

1. Plan Your Study
Before you start preparing for your exam, make a good study plan. As you create a reliable study schedule, consider your study environment, time before the test, and available resources. You must have a study plan that works best for you to manage time effectively and reduce stress. Furthermore, TEAS test study guide is available online or from nearby libraries to help you plan your TEAS exam study.

2. Take a TEAS Practice Exam
A practice exam will help you familiarize yourself with the format of the TEAS exam and the types of questions you might encounter. You can contact the admissions team or use online resources to access a TEAS practice exam. While taking the TEAS practice exam, set a timer and check the time spent on each section later.

3.Avoid Cramming
It's advisable to avoid cramming while you study for the TEAS exam. Cramming will only leave you unprepared for the exam. If you have six weeks to prepare, don't procrastinate and put yourself in a position where you're forced to cram. Instead, allow yourself at least six weeks to prepare and study for the exam.

4. Practice Time Management
Since you will have limited time for each TEAS exam section, you should know how to utilize each minute. Take your time with questions you know, and leave some extra time at the end of the exam so that you can review the answers and make corrections. One of the effective ways to practice time management is taking several practice tests and consistently trying to address any deficits when noted.

5. Trust Your Instincts
When taking the TEAS test, go with your instincts. That means you should answer every question correctly before checking multiple-choice answers. Even if the answer you were thinking of isn't one of the choices, it might help you narrow down the correct answer.

6. Answer All Questions
TEAS exams don't have penalties for wrong answers. Therefore, try to answer each question to the best of your ability. If you don't know the answer to a question, make an educated guess.

7. Calm your Mind
It's important to ease your mind and avoid stress when preparing for the TEAS exam. Take breaks to refresh your thoughts and practice self-care routines to stay relaxed. Additionally, you ought to have confidence in yourself, knowing you’re adequately prepared for the test. Ultimately, on the night before the exam, resist the urge to study overnight and try do something fun and relaxing, like watching a movie.

Wrap Up

The Test of Essential Academic Skills (TEAS) is a standardized exam that students take to test their readiness for enrollment in healthcare-related programs. TEAS exam consists of 170 multiple-choice questions covering various subject areas, and students have 209 minutes to complete the exam. The exam is divided into four sections: Reading, Mathematics, Science, and English, with each topic given its allotted time and questions.
